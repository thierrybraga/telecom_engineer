# Maxwell's Equations

## Definition
Maxwell's equations govern all classical electromagnetic phenomena.

### Differential Form
| Equation | Differential Form | Description |
|---|---|---|
| Gauss (E) | $\nabla \cdot \mathbf{E} = \rho/\varepsilon_0$ | Electric field sources |
| Gauss (B) | $\nabla \cdot \mathbf{B} = 0$ | No magnetic monopoles |
| Faraday  | $\nabla \times \mathbf{E} = -\partial\mathbf{B}/\partial t$ | Induction |
| Ampère-Maxwell | $\nabla \times \mathbf{B} = \mu_0\mathbf{J} + \mu_0\varepsilon_0\partial\mathbf{E}/\partial t$ | Current & displacement |

## Wave Equation (Vacuum)
$$\nabla^2 \mathbf{E} - \mu_0\varepsilon_0 \frac{\partial^2 \mathbf{E}}{\partial t^2} = 0$$

Speed of light: $c = 1/\sqrt{\mu_0\varepsilon_0} \approx 3\times10^8$ m/s

## Applications
- EM wave propagation in free space and guided structures
- Antenna radiation and reception
- Microwave circuit design
- Optical fiber propagation
